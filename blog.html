  • Update25 May 2020David Swan

  • Update14 April 2020David Swan

    Progress is SLOW

    1. ClearOS continues to be stable - with limitations. They want users to pay for some services so you have to be prepared to do a fair bit of digging to get what you want as opposed to what they want to give you. Samba does not work the way I think it should.

    2. Customizing Apache turned into an epic fail. ClearOS could not easily tell me directory/file ownership and permissions. The result was Apache became pretty cranky demanding user names and passwords. I got everything reset - eventually.

    3. I did get some of my favourite tools installed, thanks to those additional repositories. That was ultimately what helped me sort out Apache.

    4. Minecraft (Java) is running and stable under amp. Amp is proving a pain to restart. I have a number of options, none of which seem to work consistently. If I have to reboot the server, I download an amp update - required or not - install it and boot from it. Not pretty but it works. Current Minecraft version is 1.15.2

    More soon.
    Dave

  • Update25 December 2019David Swan

    I am not much of a blogger. I'd apologise but there has been a lot going on.

    1. There has been a full server re-load. I had made a few mistakes during my first attempt to build and run this server. You always learn what isn't in the documentation the first time around.
    2. Amp is proving to be a very solid way of running Minecraft. Updates have occurred to both amp and Minecraft seamlessly. Current version of AMP is 1.9.1.2 Current version of Minecraft is 1.15.1
    3. Overall the server, AMP and Minecraft have been stable enough that I will extending invitations to add to the website, and to Minecraft. I regularly look at new services ... but sanity prevails and I am only trying one or two things at a time. If you have a preference or suggestion please ask. File Sharing services are NOT in my plans. Too many legal issues.
    4. I should add that for a guy like me, ClearOS is proving a real winner. System overhead is very low. This server runs smoothly in the background (as a good server should), providing email, groupware, network file sharing and web services very smoothly. The automatic updates save me maintenance time. They do want to charge for security updates, but that can be avoided with a little bit of thought. AMP is providing me with the ability to add many things - however I am taking my time before I add additional services.
    Thanks for visiting. Drop me a line sometime.
    Dave

  • Political Rant18 Oct 2019David Swan

    I am disappointed. I was really looking forward to asking a lot of questions at an all-candidates forum. Most of the political parties did not answer the invitation, much less provide any information. The material available on their websites is carefully crafted nonsense. Since I have no hope of making a logical, fact based electoral decision, I thought I’d post my general observations to social media, taking note of Mark Twain’s observation:


    “Politicians and diapers must be changed often, and for the same reason.”
    Mark Twain


    First question to the Liberals and Conservatives: You have lied to me so many times over my lifetime, why should I consider voting for either of you?


    Second question for the NDP: Almost every NDP government in my lifetime has run up deficits and/or trashed the economy. Why should I trust you with the Federal economy? Especially after what Rachael Notley did to Alberta’s economy.


    And a followup: Why should I trust the unions that support the NDP any more an I trust the corporations that support the Liberal and the Conservatives?


    Third question is for the Green party: If Greenland was named that because it was GREEN and there are fossilized trees on Ellesmere Island doesn’t that that suggest there always has been climate change – and always will be. So my question is what is: green enough? We know you want to close the Oil and Gas Industry. What I don’t know is your vision … what industries do you promote? And saying green industries does not count. Be specific.


    And a follow-up: Do you understand that if you wreck the federal economy there won’t be any money to make the changes you advocate?


    Fourth question, for the People’s Party: Bernie I remember you and the “biker chick”, the secret files you left at her place, my question is can you get a security clearance? Should I take you seriously? I mean your immigration policy runs counter to Canadian experience, well English Canadian experience.


    Fifth question for the Liberals and the Conservatives: Why are relations with a mainland China, a communist government with a terrible human rights record more important than working with Taiwan, a democracy? If you don’t like that question we could discuss Russia versus Ukraine, or Georgia …


    Last question: No Canadian political party has invested seriously in the Canadian military in more than forty years. How do you intend to screw over the military and veterans this time?


    Maybe I should give the various political parties some credit. They didn’t show up for an all-candidates meeting moderated by a 38 year veteran with PTSD.
